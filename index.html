About the Public Space Invitational

Since 2014, we have sought ideas on how to make Bostonâ€™s public spaces more delightful and beautiful through the Public Space Invitational, our annual civic design competition.

This summer, we are focusing on the expansion of community compost in Boston. For several years, the City of Boston has been running Project Oscar, a program that allows residents to drop off their food scraps for compost at fives sites. We are working with Boston's Public Works Department on a significant expansion of the program by adding a dozen new community compost sites throughout Boston.

This Year's Challenge

We are seeking six teams of artists and designers to customize our standard compost bins and help us solve a number of challenges we have faced with community compost. During summer of 2021, we would like to pilot six unique bins that can address the following issues:

  • Visual Identity

    How do we make our compost bins distinct from other recycling or trash bins? Are there visual elements (such as paint) that can make them unique?

  • Accessibility

    How do we make our bins easier to use, particularly for those with disabilities?

  • Contamination

    Frequently, our compost bins are filled with trash or food scraps that are not compostable. How do we ensure that residents compost the correct items?

Applicants will be asked to address at least one of the issues above. We are looking for scalable ideas that can be implemented on one bin within six weeks. We will consider scaling successful prototypes after an evaluation period.
